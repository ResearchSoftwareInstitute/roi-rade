Teleflex division to lay off 456 in North Carolina

Arrow International, a subsidiary of Teleflex Incorporated (NYSE: TFX) in Asheboro, will lay off 456 workers, the company disclosed Friday.

The disclosure did not come as a surprise. As far back as 2014, Teleflex disclosed that it would downsize the Asheboro manufacturing operation by 2017 and relocate production to Mexico and Czech Republic. At the time, Arrow employed 635 workers.

“The Asheboro restructuring plan is a multi-phased project that will take several years to complete,” according to a memo from Teleflex human resource manager Ainsley Royals to the N.C. Department of Commerce.

Teleflex manufactures medical devices for critical care, urology and surgery. For the quarter ended March 31, Telelex reported $425 million in revenue and a net income of $51 million. Shares opened at $159.41 on Friday, giving the company a market value of nearly $7 billion.
